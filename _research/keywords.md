## High-priority content gaps

These topics have high search demand but thin or poor-quality coverage on Malaysian sites:

1. **"Nursing home vs old folks home — what's the difference"** — the single most-asked clarification; most Malaysian sites conflate the two
2. **Condition-specific landing pages** — stroke + nursing home, dementia + nursing home, Parkinson's + nursing home (users search by condition, not care type)
3. **"When does my parent need to go into a nursing home?"** — the key decision-trigger question; almost no good Malaysian content
4. **JKM subsidy / government assistance step-by-step** — "cara mohon bantuan JKM" is widely searched; government pages are confusing
5. **Cost calculator / realistic pricing by state** — "how much per month" is the second most common question after type confusion
6. **Caregiver burnout** — emotionally loaded, widely searched, zero dedicated Malaysian content

---

## Key design implication for landing page

Users arrive with one of three mental models. The landing page must route all three efficiently:

| Arrival mental model | Needs | Route to |
|---|---|---|
| "My parent has stroke / dementia / Parkinson's" | Condition → care type translation | Condition-specific guide or triage |
| "I need a nursing home in KL / Selangor" | Location picker | State/area listing |
| "I don't know what type of care my parent needs" | Triage | which-care.html quiz |
