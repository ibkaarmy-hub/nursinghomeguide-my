# Market demand & supply — Malaysia eldercare

Last updated: 2026-05-03

## Demographics (DOSM and academic sources)

- **11.6%** of Malaysians were aged 60+ in 2024, up from 5.4% in 1970.
- **65+ population**: 8.1% (2024) → projected 14.5% by 2040.
- Malaysia officially became an "ageing nation" in 2021 (≥7% aged 65+).
- Will become an "aged nation" by 2048 (≥14%).
- Source: [Ixora Senior Care 2025 trends, citing DOSM](https://ixoraseniorcare.com/blog/elderly-care-in-malaysia-trends-statistics-for-2025/)
- Source: [MOF press citation: Malaysia To Become Aged Nation By 2048](https://mof.gov.my/portal/en/news/press-citations/malaysia-to-become-aged-nation-by-2048-amir-hamzah)

## Supply

- **494 nursing homes** in Malaysia as of January 2025, **+9.43% YoY** vs 2023.
- Even with supply growth, quality facilities have **waiting lists**.
- Facilities concentrated in urban areas — rural access remains a gap.
- Trained geriatric caregivers are in short supply nationwide.
- Source: [Ixora 2025 trends](https://ixoraseniorcare.com/blog/elderly-care-in-malaysia-trends-statistics-for-2025/)

## Policy context (relevant for editorial framing)

- Malaysia has no coherent long-term care system; families remain the primary caregivers.
- This creates significant emotional, physical, and financial strain on households.
- Source: [CodeBlue (Galen Centre): Growing Old In A Young Policy State](https://codeblue.galencentre.org/2026/04/growing-old-in-a-young-policy-state/)
- Source: [UNDP Malaysia: Navigating future of care for older persons by 2040](https://www.undp.org/malaysia/blog/navigating-future-care-older-persons-malaysia-2040-community-support-technological-integration)
