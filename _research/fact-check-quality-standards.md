## Claims to verify

### Malaysia — Act 506 (Care Centres Act 1993, JKM)

1. The Care Centres Act 1993 is also known as **Act 506**.
2. Act 506 is administered by **JKM (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat / Department of Social Welfare)**, not the Ministry of Health.
3. The subsidiary legislation is the **Care Centres Regulations 1994 (P.U.(A) 248/1994)**.
4. Under JKM standards, the required caregiver-to-resident ratio is **1:18 for healthy elderly residents**.
5. Under JKM standards, the required caregiver-to-resident ratio is **1:4 for frail or abandoned elderly residents**.
6. The minimum floor space per elderly resident under JKM standards is **3.0 m²**.
7. Shared resident rooms must be a minimum of **16 m²**.
8. Common areas (dining / recreation) must provide a minimum of **4.1 m² per person**.
9. Wheelchair-accessible rooms must be a minimum of **12 m²**.
10. Caregivers must be at least **18 years old**.
11. Caregivers must complete **KAAK / KAP care training within 12 months** of starting work.
12. Caregivers must pass a **medical examination and police clearance** before employment.
13. Act 506 defines "care" as **protection, supervision, rehabilitation, and training** — not clinical / medical care.
14. Act 506 does **not require a nurse on site or a doctor on call**.
15. Act 506 does **not mandate formal protocols** for pressure injuries, falls, or medication review.
16. JKM welfare officers have authority to inspect care centres **at any time** (unannounced inspections are permitted).
17. JKM registration fee is **RM 50**, valid for **3 years**.
18. JKM registration takes approximately **21 working days** to process.
19. Approximately **361–393 facilities are registered under JKM** (vs an estimated 1,700+ operating).
20. Approximately **31% of surveyed facilities** are unable to meet the 1:18 staffing ratio.

### Malaysia — Act 586 (Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 1998, MOH)

21. Act 586 is administered by the **Ministry of Health Malaysia**.
22. The subsidiary legislation is the **Private Healthcare Facilities and Services Regulations 2006**.
23. As of October 2025, there are **approximately 18–21 facilities** licensed as nursing homes under Act 586.
24. These licensed facilities total approximately **689 beds nationally**.
25. Act 586 requires **qualified nursing staff** (personal care aides cannot perform nursing duties).
26. Act 586 requires **8-hour emergency generator fuel backup** capability.
27. Act 586 requires **emergency life-saving measures capability** on site.
28. Act 586 requires **Director General of Health approval** before a nursing home can be established.

### Malaysia — Act 802 (Private Aged Healthcare Facilities and Services Act 2018, MOH)

29. Act 802 was passed by Parliament in **November 2017**.
30. Act 802 was **gazetted on 29 March 2018**.
31. Act 802 is intended to replace Acts 506 and 586 for aged care.
32. Act 802 applies to facilities caring for **4 or more aged persons (60+)**.
33. Act 802 covers **both residential and day-care facilities**.
34. As of 2025, Act 802's implementing regulations have **not yet been approved by the Attorney General**, so the Act **cannot be fully enforced**.
35. The **Malaysia Healthy Ageing Society (MHAS)** has called for full enforcement of Act 802 since 2022.

### Malaysia — Other policy

36. The **National Ageing Framework 2025–2045** was launched in 2024.
37. The **National Dementia Action Plan** and **Health Services Action Plan for Older Persons** were also launched in 2024.
38. A **Senior Citizens Bill** is in progress as of mid-2024 but has not been tabled.

### Singapore — Healthcare Services Act 2020 + S 849/2023

39. Singapore nursing homes are regulated under the **Healthcare Services Act 2020**, not the older "Nursing Homes Act".
40. The detailed requirements are in the **Healthcare Services (Nursing Home Service) Regulations 2023 (S 849/2023)**.
41. The 2023 Regulations came into force on **18 December 2023**.
42. The Regulations require facilities to appoint a **Clinical Governance Officer** — a medical practitioner or senior RN with at least **5 years' relevant clinical experience**.
43. The Regulations require a **Head of Nursing** — a degree-qualified RN with at least 5 years' nursing care and supervisory experience.
44. Every non-hospice patient must have an **assigned attending medical practitioner** with overall responsibility for their health.
45. Every patient must have an **assigned attending registered nurse** with overall responsibility for their nursing needs.
46. A **registered nurse must be on call at all times** to support nursing personnel.
47. The Regulations require **"adequate" staffing** but do **not mandate a fixed numeric ratio** (e.g. there is no "1 RN per 15 residents" requirement in the primary legislation).
48. A written **Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) programme** is mandatory, with a dedicated IPC committee.
49. A **dietitian assessment is required** if a patient loses **≥5% of body weight over 3 months**.
50. **Price transparency** is legally mandated — fees must be displayed at the premises and disclosed before care begins.
51. Facilities must reference **MOH fee benchmarks** where available.
52. MOH separately operates the **Enhanced Nursing Home Standards (ENHS)**, introduced in **2014**, as an internal quality audit framework.
53. ENHS audit results are **not published by individual facility** — there is no public consumer-facing scorecard for Singapore nursing homes.

### Australia — ACQSC

54. The Australian aged care regulator is the **Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission (ACQSC)**.
55. Australia publishes **facility-level star ratings**.
56. The star rating is composed of 4 domains: **resident experience (33%), compliance (30%), staffing (22%), quality measures (15%)**.
57. There are **11 mandatory quarterly quality indicators**: pressure injuries, physical restraint, unplanned weight loss, falls and major injuries, medication management, activities of daily living, continence, hospitalisation, workforce turnover, consumer experience, and quality of life.
58. From **October 2023**, facilities must deliver a minimum **200 minutes of care per resident per day** (including 40 minutes of RN care).
59. From **October 2024**, this increased to **215 minutes per resident per day** (including 44 minutes RN).
60. As of **November 2025**, Australia uses **7 strengthened Quality Standards** (revised down from the previous 8).

### United Kingdom — CQC

61. The UK regulator is the **Care Quality Commission (CQC)**.
62. CQC inspects care homes against **5 key questions: Safe, Effective, Caring, Responsive, Well-led**.
63. CQC issues one of **4 ratings: Outstanding, Good, Requires Improvement, Inadequate**.
64. CQC publishes **full inspection reports** including specific evidence observed.
65. CQC inspections are typically **unannounced**.

### United States — CMS

66. The US system is called **Nursing Home Care Compare** (operated by CMS).
67. It applies a **5-star rating** to every Medicare/Medicaid-certified facility.
68. The rating aggregates **3 components: health inspections, staffing, and quality measures**.
69. There are **15 clinical quality measures**, including: fall rates, urinary tract infections, antipsychotic medication use, pressure injuries, hospitalisation, and unplanned weight loss.
70. A proposed **2024 federal rule** would mandate **1 RN per 35 residents + 0.55 nurse aide hours per resident per day**.

### Japan — MHLW / Kaigo Hoken

71. Japan's long-term care system is called **Kaigo Hoken**, administered by **MHLW**.
72. Special nursing homes (**tokubetsu yogo rojin home / tokuyo**) are required to maintain a **1:3 care staff-to-resident ratio**.
73. Japan **does not yet have a comprehensive national outcome-measurement framework** for nursing homes; quality measurement is primarily structural and still in development.
74. Japan's **pressure injury rates** in nursing homes are notably low by international standards.

## Perplexity verification results (run 2026-05-11)

Primary source checked: the Care Centres Act 1993 text (commonlii.org). Singapore claims checked against MOH 2014 ENHS announcement.

### ✅ Confirmed against primary source
- **Claim 1** — Care Centres Act 1993 = Act 506
- **Claim 2** — Administered by JKM / DG of Social Welfare (not MOH)
- **Claim 13** — Act defines "care" as protection, supervision, rehabilitation, training
- **Claim 16** — DG and authorised officers may inspect at any reasonable time → unannounced inspection authority confirmed
- **Claim 39** — Singapore nursing homes regulated under the HSA / 2023 Regulations framework, not an older standalone Nursing Homes Act
- **Claim 52** — Singapore ENHS launched in 2014

### ⚠️ NOT verifiable from primary Act text
- **Claim 3** — Care Centres Regulations 1994 P.U.(A) 248/1994 — not seen in retrieved sources
- **Claims 4, 5** — 1:18 / 1:4 caregiver ratios — Act authorises regulations on staffing but does not state ratios; would be in subsidiary regs / JKM standards doc not retrieved
- **Claims 6, 7, 8, 9** — space requirements (3.0 / 16 / 4.1 / 12 m²) — same issue
- **Claims 10, 11** — caregiver 18+ / KAAK training — Act only allows operators to set "fit and proper" conditions; no general mandatory rule for age or training in Act
- **Claim 12** — medical exam + police clearance — not in Act
- **Claim 14** — overstated: Act DOES require operators to make "arrangements for suitable medical treatment", so framing it as "no clinical mandate at all" is too strong
- **Claim 17** — RM50 fee / 3-year validity — Act mentions "prescribed fee" but not the amount
- **Claim 18** — 21 working day processing — not in Act
