## Positioning

Malaysia's **first Australian-concept aged care facility** — modelled on Australian residential aged care standards, which are internationally recognised as among the world's best. This is a genuine differentiator in the Malaysian market.

- This is a higher standard of licensing than JKM alone — CKAPS is the Ministry of Health's quality assurance programme for private healthcare facilities

## Social Worker Notes

**Strengths:**
- MOH CKAPS licensing is the gold standard in Malaysia — far exceeds basic JKM registration
- Australian-concept care model means structured care planning, resident rights focus, and regular clinical assessments
- Named medical director (Dr. Goh Yi Xiong) = accountability and clinical governance
- PEG + tracheostomy + colostomy = one of the highest clinical capability sets in Malaysia
- Surau and halal café = genuinely Muslim-friendly, not just "halal-available"
- Rooftop garden = quality of life investment, not just a clinical facility

**Gaps / Watch Points:**
- Pricing data from 2020 is stale — 5-6 years old, rates have certainly changed post-COVID
- 62 beds — mid-size facility, likely has a waiting list for the best rooms
- "Australian-concept" — confirm what this means in practice: is there a formal care planning process? Regular interdisciplinary team meetings?
- Confirm whether all clinical capabilities (trach, PEG) are available on all shifts or only during business hours

**Critical for social workers:**
- Ask specifically: what is the nurse-to-resident ratio at night?
- Ask: who manages emergency escalation at 3am — is there an RN on-site or an on-call arrangement?
- For PEG/trach patients: what is the protocol if a tube dislodges after hours?
- Visiting hours policy — not confirmed; important for family engagement

**Checklist for families:**
1. Request CKAPS licence certificate and verify on MOH website
2. Ask to meet Dr. Goh Yi Xiong or his clinical deputy during tour
3. Confirm current pricing (2020 data is outdated)
4. Ask for sample care plan to understand the "Australian model" in practice
5. Confirm overnight nursing coverage for high-dependency patients
6. Ask about waiting list for admission
7. Tour the rooftop garden and surau in person
