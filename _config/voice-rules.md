# voice-rules.md — Content Voice Guidelines

## Tone
Clear. Warm. Direct. Clinical where needed, human always.
Write like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a doctor — not like a brochure.

## Sentence Rules
- Short sentences preferred. Max 20 words per sentence.
- One idea per paragraph.
- No filler phrases ("It is important to note that...", "In conclusion...")
- Lead with the most useful information first.

## English Voice
- Use plain English. If a 14-year-old can't understand it, rewrite it.
- Avoid passive voice. "The facility accepts subsidised patients" not "Subsidised patients are accepted."
- Numbers always as digits: 12, not twelve. RM 1,200/month not "twelve hundred ringgit per month."

## Bahasa Malaysia Voice
- Use standard BM, not formal academic BM.
- Natural phrasing that mirrors how families actually search and talk.
- Key terms: rumah orang tua, pusat jagaan warga emas, yuran bulanan, kemudahan.

## What Good Looks Like (English)
> "Monthly fees range from RM 1,500 to RM 3,800 depending on room type. A shared room costs RM 1,500/month. A private room costs RM 3,800/month. Both include meals and nursing care."

## What Bad Looks Like
> "This premier establishment offers a comprehensive suite of holistic eldercare solutions at competitive market rates, tailored to the unique needs of each valued resident."

## Content Hierarchy for Facility Pages
1. Pricing (always first)
2. Location + how to get there
3. What's included
4. Who they accept (subsidy, private, religion)
5. Contact details
6. Photos
