# domain-knowledge.md — Malaysia Nursing Home Domain Facts

## Regulatory Bodies
- **JKM** (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat) — registers and licenses private nursing homes in Malaysia
- **KKM** (Kementerian Kesihatan Malaysia) — governs medical/clinical standards
- JKM maintains the official list of licensed "Rumah Orang Tua" and "Pusat Jagaan"

## Facility Types in Malaysia
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Rumah Orang Tua (ROT) | Traditional old folks home, often NGO/charity |
| Pusat Jagaan Warga Emas | Private nursing home, licensed by JKM |
| Hospital-based care | Attached to private hospitals |
| Government homes | JKM-run, subsidised, long waitlists |

## Pricing Context (Malaysia, nationwide)
- Budget (shared room): RM 800 – RM 1,500/month
- Mid-range (semi-private): RM 1,500 – RM 4,000/month
- Premium (private room): RM 4,000 – RM 12,000/month
- Government/charity homes: Free or RM 50–200/month (very limited spots)

These are nationwide ranges. The Klang Valley runs higher — see `_research/pricing-benchmarks.md` for KV-specific verified figures (reasonable KV minimum is RM 3,500+, average RM 4,000–5,000, premium from RM 7,500–8,800). Use sheet `shared_price` / `pricing_display` for facility-specific quotes; never quote a benchmark as a facility's actual price.

## Subsidy Information
- MyKasih / BKSS: welfare recipients may qualify for government homes
- SOCSO (PERKESO): limited nursing home benefit for workplace injury cases
- Families often unaware of subsidy options — explaining this is a content differentiator

## Religious / Cultural Considerations
- Muslim families often prefer homes with halal food and prayer facilities
- Chinese families often prefer Mandarin-speaking staff
- Indian families — Tamil-speaking staff valued
- These are real search filters users need — build them into the directory
