Government Assistance
for the Elderly in Malaysia

Four JKM programs that most families don't know about — including RM600/month cash for low-income elderly and RM500/month paid directly to carers of bedridden family members.

✓ Up to RM600/month cash (BWE)
RM500/month for carers (BPT)
Free day centres (PAWE)
Free home help (KBDR)
⚠️ Most families are missing out on assistance they qualify for

The four government programs below are run by JKM (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat) and cost nothing to apply for. Many families caring for an elderly parent at home — including those with a bedridden parent — qualify for monthly cash payments and free services but have never been told they exist. This guide explains each program in plain language and tells you exactly how to apply.

👨‍⚕️ A Doctor's Perspective
👨‍⚕️
Written by a practising Malaysian doctor Founder, NursingHomeGuide.my

In my clinical work, I regularly see families who are exhausted and financially stretched caring for an elderly parent — and who have no idea that the government provides financial support for exactly this situation. The BPT program in particular pays RM500 per month directly to the carer of a bedridden family member. I have met many families who have been providing this care for years without ever claiming it.

This is not a handout — it is a recognition that family caregivers provide a vital service that would otherwise fall on the healthcare system. If you are looking after a parent who cannot move without help, check whether you qualify for BPT before you do anything else on this page.

The other programs listed here — especially the free PAWE day centres — are also significantly underused. A parent who is still mobile but lonely and isolated at home can attend a PAWE centre five days a week at no cost. That social engagement is genuinely protective against cognitive decline and depression in the elderly.

Bantuan Warga Emas (BWE) — Elderly Financial Assistance

RM600/month Cash for Low-Income Elderly

RM600 per month, paid directly to your parent

Who qualifies

  • Malaysian citizen aged 60 years or above
  • Household income falls within the Garis Kemiskinan Tegar (Hardcore Poor Poverty Line) — typically below RM1,169/month for Peninsula Malaysia
  • Currently living in the community (not in a government-funded residential institution or hospital)
  • Does not already receive another JKM residential placement

How to apply

  • Visit the nearest JKM district office (Pejabat Kebajikan Masyarakat Daerah) — or apply online at ebantuanjkm.jkm.gov.my
  • Complete Form JKM18 (Borang Permohonan Bantuan Am)
  • Bring: MyKad (original + photocopy), proof of income, bank account details
  • A JKM officer will conduct a home visit to verify living conditions
  • Processing takes approximately 4–8 weeks once documentation is complete
Apply online at JKM →

Important notes

BWE is income-tested — your parent must fall within the government's poverty classification to qualify. Middle-income families are unlikely to qualify, but lower-income or single-parent households where an elderly person lives on savings or a small pension may meet the threshold. The RM600 is paid monthly into your parent's bank account. If your parent does not have a bank account, this can be arranged through BSN (Bank Simpanan Nasional) when applying.

Bantuan Penjagaan Terlantar (BPT) — Bedridden Carer Allowance

RM500/month Paid to the Family Carer

RM500 per month, paid to the person caring for them — not the patient
This program is the one most families don't know about

If you are at home caring for a parent who is bedridden or severely disabled, the government recognises this as a real caring role and pays you RM500/month for it. This money is paid to you as the carer — not to your parent. It does not affect your parent's eligibility for other assistance.

Who qualifies — the carer

  • A Malaysian citizen caring for a bedridden or severely disabled person at home
  • The disabled/ill person must be a family member (spouse, parent, child, sibling)
  • The carer must be providing care full-time at home — not in a nursing home or care centre
  • Household income generally below RM3,000–RM5,000/month (varies by district office assessment)
  • The person being cared for must have a medical letter confirming the condition

How to apply

  • Go to the nearest JKM district office — this cannot be done fully online; a home visit is required
  • Bring: MyKad for both the carer and the person being cared for
  • Bring a medical letter from a doctor confirming the diagnosis and bedbound status (ask your parent's GP or specialist for this)
  • Bring proof of household income (payslips, bank statements)
  • A JKM officer will visit your home to verify the situation
  • If approved, RM500/month is paid into the carer's bank account

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Applying without a doctor's letter first — this slows processing significantly
  • Not realising the carer receives the money (not the patient) — many families apply with the wrong person's bank account
  • Assuming income is too high — JKM assesses total household expenses against income, and families with heavy care costs often qualify even at moderate income levels
  • Giving up after a single visit — if your case is borderline, request a reassessment or speak to a JKM social worker

Who qualifies — the person being cared for

  • Bedridden due to stroke, dementia, cancer, neurological disease, or severe physical disability
  • Cannot perform basic activities of daily living independently (bathing, dressing, eating, toileting)
  • Requires daily assistance or medical care
  • Is a Malaysian citizen
  • Is currently living at home — not in a nursing home or hospital

Pusat Aktiviti Warga Emas (PAWE) — Government Senior Activity Centres

Free Day Centres for Mobile Seniors — 190+ Locations Nationwide

Free no charge to attend; over 190 centres nationwide

What PAWE offers

  • Physical activities — morning exercises, light aerobics, stretching
  • Social interaction — group activities, peer connection, events
  • Arts, crafts & skills — painting, weaving, gardening, cooking
  • Health screening — some centres offer basic blood pressure and health checks
  • Nutritious meals — some centres provide subsidised meals
  • Typically open Monday to Friday, 8am–5pm

Who qualifies & how to find a centre

  • Malaysian aged 60 years or above
  • Medically stable and mobile — can travel to the centre independently or with family
  • No income requirement — open to all elderly Malaysians regardless of income
  • Search for your nearest PAWE at online.jkm.gov.my/pawe or call the JKM hotline 03-8000 8000
  • Register through the centre directly — bring MyKad and a recent passport photo

Program Khidmat Bantu Di Rumah (KBDR) — Home Help Volunteer Service

Free Trained Volunteers Who Visit Your Parent at Home

Free trained volunteers visit the home; launched July 2012

What KBDR volunteers help with

  • Personal hygiene (bathing, grooming, oral care)
  • Meal preparation and feeding
  • Medication reminders and instructions
  • Light housekeeping
  • Companionship and social engagement
  • Escorting to the bank, market, or clinic
  • Medical appointment transport and escort
  • Basic physiotherapy exercises (if volunteer is qualified)

Who qualifies & how to apply

  • Elderly aged 60+ or a person with a disability (OKU) living at home
  • Limited family support available (family is working, living far away, or also elderly)
  • Living in an area where KBDR is operating — coverage varies by district
  • Apply by contacting the nearest JKM district office directly
  • Or email: pro_jkm@jkm.gov.my
  • A JKM social worker will assess the situation and match a volunteer

Realistic expectations

KBDR is a supplementary support service — it is not a substitute for a paid carer, nurse, or day care centre. Visits are typically a few times per week, not daily. The service is best used alongside other support: a family member who provides care in the evenings and weekends, with KBDR filling gaps during the week. If your parent requires daily medical care, professional home nursing (from a private agency) or a day care centre will be necessary.

Other programs worth knowing

Rumah Seri Kenangan (RSK) — Government residential care homes

Government-operated residential homes for elderly Malaysians with no family support or income. Subsidised or free for those who qualify. Very limited capacity and long waiting lists. Apply through the JKM district office. Not suitable as a short-term solution due to wait times.

Tabung Bantuan Perubatan (TBP) — Medical relief fund

Covers up to RM50,000 or more for serious medical conditions including cardiac, renal, and neurological diseases. Requires a referral from a government hospital doctor. Applied through the hospital's medical social worker (pegawai kebajikan perubatan).

Skim Mesra Usia Emas (SMUE) — Selangor state scheme

For Selangor residents aged 60+ (MyKad address must be in Selangor). Provides RM100 annually in grocery aid and RM500 one-off for funeral costs. Apply at e-mesra.yawas.my or at your DUN service centre. Call YAWAS Careline: 03-5481 8800.

Government nursing homes (Rumah Seri Kenangan) are run by JKM directly for elderly with no family or income. They are free or heavily subsidised but have very limited capacity and long waiting lists. JKM-registered care centres (under Act 506) are privately run facilities that are licensed by JKM — they are not government facilities and charge market rates. Only 18 facilities in Malaysia are licensed as nursing homes under MOH Act 586 and can legally provide clinical medical care. Most facilities families find online are Act 506 care centres, not Act 586 nursing homes.
