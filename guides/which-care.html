Understanding your options
🏘️ Assisted Living

For people who are still largely self-sufficient. They can bathe, dress, and eat on their own — they just want company, cooked meals, and someone nearby for peace of mind.

🏥 Nursing Home

For people who need hands-on help with bathing, dressing, eating, or moving — or who have ongoing medical needs such as wound care, dementia management, or post-stroke recovery.

How much help does your loved one need with daily tasks?

🏡 Professional Home Care

Care comes to your loved one. A trained caregiver or visiting nurse handles personal care, medication, and light nursing at home — keeping them in familiar surroundings.

💰
From RM29–RM40/hour or RM2,500/month for regular packages.

Why this fits

  • Your loved one's independence is preserved — residential care would remove that unnecessarily.
  • Start with a few hours per day and step up as needs grow. Moving to a nursing home later is always an option.

☀️ Day Care Centre

Your loved one attends during working hours and returns home each evening — staying socially engaged and supervised while family is at work.

💰
Free at government PAWE centres, or RM80–RM125/day at private day care.

Why this fits

  • Provides structured activity, meals, and supervision without uprooting your loved one from home.
  • Government PAWE centres are free for mobile seniors aged 60+. Family stays close.

🏘️ Assisted Living

Residential care without intensive nursing. Your loved one lives in a community with peers, with 24-hour staff for daily support — but keeps meaningful independence.

💰
RM2,500–RM8,500/month standard tier; premium communities go higher.

Why this fits

  • Your loved one needs more support and company than home life provides, but does not have complex medical needs requiring nursing care.
  • The lifestyle matters here — dining, social calendar, amenities — not just clinical capability.

Best fit for your situation

🏥 Nursing Home

Your loved one's medical or care needs have reached a level where round-the-clock nursing is the right answer. A licensed nursing home is the appropriate next step.

⚠️ Verify the licence before committing. Most Malaysian facilities are JKM-registered (Act 506). Only a small number hold a full MOH nursing home licence (Act 586) authorising clinical medical procedures. If your loved one needs tube feeding, wound care, catheter management, or other nursing procedures, ask to see the specific licence on the tour.
💰
RM2,000–RM8,000/month standard to skilled nursing. Room type, care level, and location affect the price significantly.

Why this fits

  • The clinical or supervisory needs exceed what home care or assisted living can safely deliver.
  • This is a responsible call, not a failure — professional 24-hour nursing care is safer than struggling at home for complex needs.
