Which Care Does My Parent Actually Need?

Three quick questions. We'll point you to the right category and the directory of facilities to start with.

📋 Before You Start

Most Malaysian families only consider nursing homes — but they're often the wrong starting point. Professional home care, a day care centre, or assisted living can be a better fit, and costs less. This tool routes you to the category that matches the actual situation.

🏡 Professional Home Care

Care comes to your parent. A trained caregiver or visiting nurse handles personal care, medication, and light nursing at home — keeping your parent in their familiar surroundings.

💰
From RM29–RM40/hour or RM2,500/month for regular packages.
Most providers cover all of Klang Valley + Johor; some go nationwide.

Why this fits

  • Your parent has independence to preserve — residential care would remove that unnecessarily.
  • You can start with a few hours per day and step up as needs grow. If they grow significantly, you can move to assisted living or a nursing home later.

Best fit for your situation

☀️ Day Care Centre

Your parent attends during working hours and returns home each evening — staying socially engaged and supervised while family is at work.

💰
Free at government PAWE centres, or RM80–RM125/day at private day care.
Significantly cheaper than residential care; preserves home life.

Why this fits

  • Your parent is mobile enough to attend a centre and benefits from structured activity and peer interaction.
  • Family stays close — no relocation. Government PAWE centres are free for mobile seniors aged 60+.

Best fit for your situation

🏘️ Assisted Living

Residential care without intensive nursing. Your parent lives in a community with peers, with 24-hour staff for daily tasks — but keeps meaningful independence.

💰
RM2,500–RM8,500/month standard tier; premium communities go higher.
More affordable and life-affirming than a nursing home if clinical needs are still light.

Why this fits

  • Your parent needs more support than home care can give, but doesn't have complex medical needs that require 24-hour nursing.
  • The community shape matters — dining, social calendar, amenities — not just clinical capability.

Best fit for your situation

🏥 Nursing Home

Your parent's medical or cognitive needs have reached a level where round-the-clock nursing care is the right answer. A licensed nursing home — not assisted living — is the appropriate next step.

⚠️ Verify the licence before committing. Most Malaysian facilities are JKM-registered (Act 506). Only ~18 facilities hold a full MOH nursing home licence (Act 586) authorising clinical medical care. If your parent needs tube feeding, wound care, catheter management or other nursing procedures, ask for the specific licence on the tour.
💰
RM2,000–RM8,000/month standard to skilled nursing.
Room type, care level, and location move the price significantly.

Why this fits

  • Your parent's clinical or supervisory needs exceed what home care or assisted living can safely deliver.
  • This is a responsible call, not a failure — professional 24-hour care is safer than struggling at home for complex needs.
